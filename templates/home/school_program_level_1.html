WHAT ARE THE MODULES IN THE LEVEL I SCHOOL OF PSYCHOLOGY?
MODULE I – Introduction and History of Psychology
- Roots of Psychology
- Modern psychology
- Object and Subject of Psychology
- Subdivisions of Psychology
- Psychology with psychotherapy
- Psychology: Practice and Science
- Types of therapeutic schools

MODULE II – Theories of Personality I
- What is a personality?
- Freud's theory of personality
- Jung's theory of personality
- Skinner's theory of personality

MODULE III – Theories of Personality II
- Personality: Adler's Theory
- Dispositional theories of personality - Eysenck
- Humanistic theories of personality - Maslow

MODULE IV – Child psychology and developmental psychology
- Developmental Psychology
- Attachment Theory - Bowlby
- Psychosexual development according to Freud
- Erik Erikson and Creasy in Personality Development
- Stages of cognitive development - Piaget

MODULE V – Family Systems
- The family as a system
- Family structure
- Family life cycle
- Stages of development and crises in the family

MODULE VI – Theory of Communication
- Why do we communicate?
- Elements in communication
- Types of communication
- Types of listening

MODULE VII – Psychology of Lying
- Why do we all lie?
- How can we recognize a lie?
- Are there indicators of fraudulent behavior?
- Would we like to have this skill?
- How to build trust?

MODULE VIII – Psychopathology
- Psychopathology and psychology
- Diagnosing
- Anxiety disorders
- Affective disorders
- Psychoses
- Eating disorders

MODULE XI – Neuropsychology I
- The nervous system
- Structure and lobes of the brain
- Functions of the brain
- Limbic system

MODULE X – Neuropsychology II
- The cells in the brain
- Communication in the brain
- Neurotransmitters
- Endocrine system

MODULE XI – Emotional Intelligence
- Emotional brain
- Emotion Vs. Feeling
- Basic human emotions
- Functions of emotions
- Empathy
- Assertiveness

MODULE XII – The most famous theories in Psychology
- Social Psychology - Zimbardo, Milgram, Dunbar
- Cognitive psychology - selective attention
- Psychology of advertising
- Placebo/Nocebo
